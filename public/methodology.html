Accuracy & Methodology

How we calculate pet birth charts

Little Souls computes every pet's planetary placements using the VSOP87 planetary theory — the same high-precision ephemeris model professional astronomers use — accurate to within arc-seconds for the modern era. We never guess or fabricate a placement. Sun-sign dates use the tropical zodiac; the Moon uses the ELP2000 lunar theory. The astrology is for insight; the astronomy is real.

The ephemeris engine

A birth chart is a snapshot of where every body in the solar system sat, from Earth's view, at a precise moment. Getting that snapshot right is an astronomy problem, not a guess. We use the VSOP87 planetary theory (Bretagnon & Francou, 1988) via the open-source astronomia library to compute the heliocentric, then geocentric, ecliptic longitude of each planet, and the ELP2000 lunar theory for the Moon.

  • Sun & planets — VSOP87B series, converted to apparent geocentric longitude with nutation applied.
  • Moon — ELP2000-82B, the standard high-accuracy lunar model.
  • Chiron — computed from a precomputed positional table, because Chiron's high orbital eccentricity makes simple analytic models unreliable.
  • Time zones — birth moments are normalised to UTC using the historical zone for the birth location, so a placement never drifts by a sign because of a time-zone error.

From positions to signs

Each body's ecliptic longitude (0–360°) maps to one of the twelve tropical signs of 30° each. This is the same convention used across Western astrology and is independent of the slowly precessing constellations.

When the birth time is unknown

Most placements — Sun, Moon, and the planets — depend only on the date and barely move within a day, so they are reliable even without a birth time. The rising sign and house cusps do need a time. When it is unknown, we use a transparent solar-chart convention and say so, rather than inventing a time and presenting a false rising sign.

Rescue & adoption pets

Many pets have no known birthday. Astrology has a long-standing convention for unknown origins: anchor the chart to the moment a new chapter began. We use the adoption or "gotcha" date as a soul birthday — an honest, meaningful anchor rooted in your actual bond.

What we will never do

  • Fabricate a degree, sign or placement to fit a narrative.
  • Present a rising sign when no birth time exists.
  • Round away the astronomy to make the story neater.

Accuracy is the foundation the meaning rests on. The interpretation is crafted to be insightful and is offered for reflection and entertainment — but it is always built on a correctly computed chart.

Frequently asked

Are pet birth charts accurate?

The astronomy is accurate to arc-seconds via VSOP87. The astrological reading is interpretive and for insight and entertainment — but it is always built on a rigorously computed chart, not a generic template.

What if I don't know my pet's exact birth time?

Sun, Moon and planets need only the date. The rising sign needs a time; when it's unknown we use a solar-chart convention and tell you, rather than guessing.

How are rescue or adoption dates handled?

The adoption or "gotcha" date is used as a soul birthday — a standard astrological convention for unknown origins, anchored to a real moment in your bond.
